<div class="card">
<h3>ROI Grade: A+</h3>
<p><strong>Timing:</strong> March–June and September–November (Annual)</p>
<p><strong>Locations:</strong> Chicago, TX, Northeast, Midwest, Southeast</p>
<p><strong>Website:</strong> https://www.d2p.com</p>

<h4>Industries Attending</h4>
<ul>
<li>Pharmaceutical Manufacturing</li>
<li>Nutraceuticals</li>
<li>Medical Devices</li>
<li>Powder Metallurgy</li>
<li>Advanced Materials</li>
<li>Automotive Suppliers</li>
<li>Aerospace & Defense</li>
<li>Contract Manufacturers (CMOs)</li>
<li>Precision Machining</li>
<li>Metal Forming & Compaction</li>
<li>Additive Manufacturing</li>
</ul>

<h4>Buyer Personas</h4>
<ul>
<li>Manufacturing Engineers</li>
<li>Process Engineers</li>
<li>Tooling Engineers</li>
<li>Operations Managers</li>
<li>Plant Managers</li>
</ul>

<h4>What They’re Dealing With</h4>
<p>
Active production programs, tooling wear, tolerance drift, startup scrap, yield loss, press downtime, and scale-up failures where lab results break under production pressure.
</p>

<h4>How Natoli Fits</h4>
<p>
Natoli’s tablet tooling, punch and die systems, and compaction expertise directly translate into powder metallurgy, precision forming, and advanced material environments where tooling stability drives yield and uptime.
</p>

<h4>How ROI Is Created</h4>
<p>
Engineers arrive with live programs and drawings. Tooling specs and tolerances are discussed on the floor. Feasibility is validated immediately. This leads to RFQs, trials, validation work, plant visits, and multi-year tooling programs with short sales cycles because the pain already exists.
</p>

<h4>Estimated Cost</h4>
<ul>
<li>Booth and Space: $6k–$10k</li>
<li>Travel and Lodging: $4k–$6k</li>
<li>Materials and Labor: $3k–$5k</li>
<li><strong>Total Estimated Cost:</strong> $13k–$21k</li>
</ul>

<h4>ROI Measurement</h4>
<p>
Qualified engineering conversations, RFQs initiated, trials launched, plant visits scheduled, and revenue influenced within 3–9 months.
</p>

<h4>Why This Show Makes Sense</h4>
<p>
This is Natoli’s highest-probability environment because the buyers already compress, compact, or form materials and are accountable for tooling performance.
</p>
</div>
