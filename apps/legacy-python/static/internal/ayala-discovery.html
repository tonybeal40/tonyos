Current Website Messaging

What They Say

"Clean Water Without Energy, Chemicals, or Heavy Infrastructure. Nature-based systems that reduce operating cost, regulatory risk, and carbon footprint."

The Opportunity

The messaging focuses on features (nature-based, no chemicals) rather than the buyer's internal problem. Decision-makers in municipal and industrial water management are under pressure from regulators, budgets, and stakeholders. The website could connect more directly to those pain points before introducing the solution.

Website Strengths

  • +
    Clear Value Proposition Immediately communicates the core benefit: water treatment without energy or chemicals.
  • +
    Strong Industry Segmentation Municipal, Industrial, Remote Communities, Developers - each addressed with specific pain points.
  • +
    Low-Friction CTA "Request Technical Review" is non-threatening and appropriate for the sales cycle.
  • +
    Environmental Positioning Carbon reduction and sustainability messaging aligns with buyer priorities.

Messaging Gaps Identified

  • !
    Missing the Internal Villain What keeps the water manager up at night? Regulatory pressure, budget scrutiny, public trust? These emotions are absent.
  • !
    No Guide Positioning Ayala doesn't position itself as the expert partner who has solved this before. Where's the authority?
  • !
    Failure Stakes Undefined What happens if they do nothing? Regulatory fines? Service disruptions? Political fallout? The cost of inaction is unclear.
  • !
    Success Vision Weak The "after" state is vague. Paint the picture: compliance confidence, budget surplus, community praise.
  • !
    No Case Studies or Proof Points Claims without evidence. Buyers need to see real implementations, real results, real names.
