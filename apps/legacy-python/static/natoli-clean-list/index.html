⚙️ The Basic Tablet Compression Cycle

Reference guide for Natoli sales reps. Know the machine, know the customer's pain points.

PUNCH & DIE TERMINOLOGY

Upper Punch: Head Flat, Head, Neck, Barrel, Key Slot, Barrel Chamfer, Tip Length, Tip
Lower Punch: Head, Neck, Barrel, Tip, Key Slot, Overall Length
Die: Die OD, Die Lock, Chamber, Die Bore

COMPRESSION STAGES

Filling: Lower punches pulled down by fill cam, powder flows into die bore
Compression: Powder compressed between upper and lower punches via main compression rolls, air expelled
Ejection: Lower punch pushed up by ejection cam, take-off blade pushes tablet out of die

KEY COMPONENTS

Feeder: Distributes powder into dies
Scraper: Removes excess powder
Pre-Compression Rolls: Initial compaction, removes air
Main Compression Rolls: Final compaction force
Weight Cam: Controls fill depth
Take-Off Blade: Guides finished tablet away

COMMON TOOLING OPTIONS

Domed Head: Extended contact with compression roll for smoother transition
Rotating Punch Head: Reduces head wear, extends tool life
Multiple Tip Punch: Produces multiple tablets per station
Dust Cup Groove: Sealing device to prevent powder contamination
Double Deep Relief: For tablets requiring deep cup depths
Bakelite Relief: Reduces contact area between die and punch tip

ADDITIONAL TOOLING

Shortened Tip Length: For shallow-fill applications
Undercut Die: Reduces ejection force
Short Lower Punch Tip Width: Accommodates thin tablet designs
Hi-Pro Woodruff Key: High-profile key for better alignment
Fixed Parallel Key: Maintains consistent orientation
Lined Die: Carbide-lined for abrasive formulations
Tapered Die: Tapered bore for easier ejection

SALES TIP: When talking to a process engineer, reference their specific stage pain points. If they mention sticking or capping, pivot to tooling coatings and tip design. If they mention weight variation, talk about fill cam and feeder optimization. Natoli solves every stage of this cycle.

Common Tablet Defects

Know the defect, know the fix, own the conversation. Every defect below is a Natoli tooling or process opportunity.

STICKING

Probable Causes: Punch face condition, granulation issues, excessive moisture, unsuitable lubrication
Corrective Actions: Polish punch faces, check moisture content of granulation, apply anti-stick coatings, consider chrome or DLC-coated tooling

PICKING

Probable Causes: Punch face condition, excessive moisture, temperature/humidity, different formulation
Corrective Actions: Polish or coat punch faces, check moisture content, adjust granulation particle size, consider embossing design changes

CAPPING

Probable Causes: Air entrapment, excess fines, worn or rough die bore, excessive pre-compression
Corrective Actions: Reduce press speed, add pre-compression, reduce final compression, check die condition, taper die bore

LAMINATION

Probable Causes: Excessive pre-compression, rapid decompression, formulation issues, worn tooling
Corrective Actions: Reduce pre-compression, slow press speed, modify granulation, check punch tip condition

CHIPPING

Probable Causes: Punch face worn, burrs on tools, take-off blade set too close
Corrective Actions: Inspect and polish tooling, set take-off blade properly, ensure punch keys are properly aligned

SPLITTING

Probable Causes: Over-compression, inadequate pressure from pre-compression
Corrective Actions: Reduce compression force, increase pre-compression, reduce fines

DISCOLORATION

Probable Causes: Colorant migration, poor tablet design, cross-contamination
Corrective Actions: Check colorant distribution, clean all equipment thoroughly, verify formulation consistency

DARK SPECS

Probable Causes: Lubricant on upper punch, cross-contamination from another product
Corrective Actions: Clean equipment between batches, inspect raw materials, check punch tips for wear

SCORCHED BELLY BAND

Probable Causes: Excessive friction between tablet and die wall during ejection
Corrective Actions: Minimize depth of fill, add more lubricant, taper die bore

EDGE EROSION

Probable Causes: Unequal distribution of granulation, poor tablet design
Corrective Actions: Set scraper correctly, modify granulation particle distribution

EXCESSIVE FLASHING

Probable Causes: Worn die bore, punch tip-to-die clearance too large
Corrective Actions: Replace worn dies, verify proper punch-to-die fit, reduce compression

DOUBLE IMPRESSION & TWINNING

Double Impression: Punch rotates during compression (round tablets). Fix with anti-turning devices and proper keying
Twinning: Two tablets stuck together. Adjust take-off blade, check ejection timing
Thickness/Weight Deviation: Inconsistent fill depth. Check weight cam, feeder speed, granulation flow

SALES TIP: When a prospect mentions ANY of these defects, you have an opening. Every defect maps to a Natoli solution — coated tooling for sticking/picking, tapered dies for capping/scorching, precision-ground tips for weight variation. Ask: "What's your biggest headache on the press right now?" Then match the fix.
