      <p><strong>Short answer: Yes. These accounts absolutely work for Natoli.</strong></p>
      
      <p>They just don't work the way people expect trade shows to work. What you're seeing is not a red flag. <strong>It's actually the signal.</strong></p>
      
      <h4 style="margin: 24px 0 12px; color: var(--white);">Why These Accounts Work:</h4>
      
      <p><strong>1. Buyers are operators, not shoppers.</strong><br>
      Manufacturing Engineers, Process Engineers, Tooling Owners, Plant Managers. These people own tooling performance, get blamed for downtime/scrap/yield loss, and influence RFQs before procurement gets involved.</p>
      
      <p><strong>2. Competitor absence is a feature, not a flaw.</strong><br>
      If competitors were there, conversations would shift to price. Specs would be frozen. Decisions would already be downstream. Instead, problems are still being defined, tooling assumptions are still open, and engineering credibility matters more than logos.</p>
      
      <p><strong>3. These shows feed higher-value downstream outcomes.</strong><br>
      What happens after the show: Plant invitations, drawings and tolerances shared, feasibility opinions requested, trials and validation work triggered, multi-year tooling programs established.</p>
      
      <p><strong>4. Why pharma OEMs aren't there (and why that's fine).</strong><br>
      Big OEMs chase capital budgets and executive visibility. Natoli's value is tool life, yield stability, process repeatability, uptime. Different layer of the stack.</p>
    </div>
