The Framework

Our 5-step alignment loop

Most Sales and Marketing friction comes from different definitions of "qualified" and different stories being told to the same buyer.

01

Agree on the buyer question

Pick one real customer question to answer this week. No multipurpose messaging.

02

Publish the message once

One core message, reused across email, enablement, and conversations.

03

Capture intent signals

Sales is activated only when intent is clear, not when activity looks good.

04

Route with context

Interest is routed to the right owner with context. No dropped balls.

05

Feedback loop

What buyers actually said becomes the next message iteration.

Our Lead Generation Process

How we build and qualify pipeline

1. Targeted List BuildingLists built from ICP criteria, not purchased databases.
  • 25+ validated Natoli industries
  • Company size, growth stage, geography filters
  • Manual review before pipeline entry
  • Apollo enrichment for technographics
2. Persona VettingOnly decision-makers who can move deals forward.
  • Director of R&D, VP Manufacturing, MS&T leads
  • Title-to-responsibility validation
  • Division routing: Scientific vs Engineering
  • Gatekeepers and researchers excluded
3. Multi-Channel OutreachLinkedIn + Email sequences designed for replies.
  • LinkedIn connection with value-first message
  • 2-3 follow-up touches based on engagement
  • Email sequence for non-responders
  • Personalized by company context
4. Intent-Based TieringEvery action scored and prioritized for Sales.
  • Tier 1: Replied or clicked - immediate action
  • Tier 2: Opened/engaged - follow-up queue
  • Tier 3: No signal - nurture or remove
Handoff StandardEvery lead passed to Sales includes: Who (name, company, role), What (trigger message), Why (problem signal), Seen (content reviewed), and Next (recommended angle).

90-Day Implementation Roadmap

Getting to full alignment

Days 1-30: Foundation

Align on ICP definition. Establish weekly sync cadence. Document handoff template. Review current pipeline together. Set baseline metrics.

Days 31-60: Execution

Run first coordinated campaign with shared messaging. Implement feedback loop (Sales to Marketing). Refine tier definitions based on real data. First monthly pipeline review.

Days 61-90: Optimization

Analyze first campaign results. Update messaging based on objections. Expand to second campaign angle. Conduct first win/loss review. Document what's working.

Success at Day 90Both teams using same ICP definition. Weekly sync happening consistently. Handoffs include context. Sales feedback improving next campaign. Pipeline visibility is shared.

When Things Break Down

Escalation paths and fixes

Sales not following upCheck: Is context clear? Is timing realistic? Discuss in weekly sync. Adjust handoff format if needed.
Marketing sending unqualified leadsCheck: Are tier definitions aligned? Review rejected leads together. Tighten qualification criteria.
No feedback coming backMake feedback part of CRM workflow. Add to weekly sync agenda. Keep it simple: Win / Not Now / Bad Fit.
Message not resonatingReview objections from Sales. Test new angle in small batch. Iterate weekly, not monthly.
