        <h2>The framework</h2>
        <p>
          Most Sales and Marketing friction comes from two things: different definitions of a qualified opportunity and different stories being told to the same buyer.
          The fix is a simple loop with clear ownership.
        </p>

        <ol class="steps">
          <li class="step">
            <div class="label"><span class="num">01</span> Agree on the buyer question</div>
            <p class="desc">Pick one real customer question to answer this week. No multipurpose messaging.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="step">
            <div class="label"><span class="num">02</span> Publish the message once</div>
            <p class="desc">One core message, reused across email, enablement, and conversations.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="step">
            <div class="label"><span class="num">03</span> Capture intent signals</div>
            <p class="desc">Sales is activated only when intent is clear, not when activity looks good.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="step">
            <div class="label"><span class="num">04</span> Route responses to action</div>
            <p class="desc">Interest is routed to the right owner with context. No dropped balls.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="step">
            <div class="label"><span class="num">05</span> Capture objections and reality</div>
            <p class="desc">What buyers actually said, and what stalled deals. That becomes the next iteration.</p>
          </li>
        </ol>
      </section>

      <h2>How information is passed to Sales</h2>
      <p>
        Sales gets involved only when buyer intent is explicit. When that happens, Marketing passes a short context brief.
        It is not a lead score. It is context that lets Sales continue the same conversation.
      </p>

      <div>
        <span class="badge">Trigger: reply</span>
        <span class="badge">Trigger: click on a specific asset</span>
        <span class="badge">Trigger: form question</span>
      </div>

      <div style="height:12px"></div>

      <div class="two">
        <div class="mini">
          <strong>Marketing packages the context</strong>
          <span>The handoff is always the same, so it is fast to read and easy to act on.</span>
          <ul class="list">
            <li><strong>Who</strong>: name, company, role</li>
            <li><strong>What</strong>: the exact message or topic that triggered interest</li>
            <li><strong>Why</strong>: the question, objection, or problem signal</li>
            <li><strong>Seen</strong>: what the buyer already reviewed</li>
            <li><strong>Next</strong>: one recommended angle, optional</li>
          </ul>
        </div>

        <div class="mini">
          <strong>Sales continues the same conversation</strong>
          <span>Sales does not reset the narrative. Sales follows up with relevance and control.</span>
          <ul class="list">
            <li>Reference the topic they engaged with</li>
            <li>Ask one clarifying question</li>
            <li>Advance to the right next step</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div style="height:12px"></div>

      <div class="mini">
        <strong>Closed loop feedback</strong>
        <span>After the interaction, Sales shares what moved the conversation forward or what stalled it. That input shapes the next message cycle.</span>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Interested: advance and note what resonated</li>
          <li>Not now: capture timing and reason</li>
          <li>Not relevant: capture why, so targeting improves</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </section>
