Positioning snapshot

EPM is a pan-European outlet that blends equipment suppliers, technology providers and general manufacturing stories. It gives Natoli regional exposure to production teams and buyers across Europe, but the audience is more mixed than APR or Tablets & Capsules.

What EPM is
  • Manufacturing-oriented coverage with regular visibility for machinery, components and technology vendors.
  • A channel where European plant managers, engineering leaders and project teams can see tooling and press brands in context.
  • Good support vehicle for reinforcing Natoli’s name around other Tier 1 pharma campaigns.
What EPM is not
  • It is not a pure solid-dose tablet press and tooling hub.
  • It is not the place where MS&T and formulation teams go first for deep compaction science.
  • It is not a standalone pipeline engine. On its own, it will not generate consistent SQL-level opportunities.

Bottom line for Natoli
If the goal is pure pipeline: EPM should never replace Tier 1 channels. It can assist, not lead.

If the goal is European brand presence: EPM helps keep Natoli visible alongside other manufacturing brands across the EU market.

If the goal is strategic authority: use selectively for case studies and plant-level stories that show Natoli solving production challenges.

How Natoli should use EPM
  • Position EPM as a reinforcement channel when Natoli is already active in APR, T&C and other Tier 1 titles.
  • Use it to support plant-level stories, European case studies and reliability themes.
  • Align calls to action with higher intent channels: web forms, webinars, or CT/compaction content that lives on Natoli’s own properties.
  • Avoid over-investing in pure banners; concentrate on placements where Natoli’s story can be told with context.

Executive summary for leadership

Decision statement
EPM is a solid Tier 2 European visibility channel. It keeps Natoli present in the manufacturing conversation, but it does not replace Tier 1 pharma engines for serious compaction work.

Practical interpretation:
  • Fund EPM only after core Tier 1 channels are secured.
  • Cap EPM spend so it never dilutes budget away from APR, Tablets & Capsules or other high-density buyer hubs.
  • Measure success in terms of European awareness, not raw SQL count.
